# Design & Campaign Notes

## Candidate

- **Nabil Zaghloul** — 30 years as a county executive, late 50s, looks relatively young
- Running for **Multnomah County Commissioner, District 2**
- Positioning: institutional knowledge + insider credibility to fix things from within
- Not a grassroots insurgent — a serious person with a record
- The name is foreign to all demographic groups in the district. Design makes it feel normal, not exotic.

## District 2 Demographics

- **North and Northeast Portland** — ~160,000 residents
- 65.4% White, 12.5% Hispanic, 8.4% Black, 7.4% Pacific Islander, 4.0% Asian
- Portland's most diverse district; historically the center of the Black community
- 61% Democrat, 29% non-affiliated, 5% Republican
- Median household income ~$94,000
- 14.4% of households speak a non-English language at home

## Election Context

- **November 3, 2026** — election day (no primary — straight to general with RCV)
- **Ranked choice voting** — first time for county commissioner races
- Filing period: June 3 – August 25, 2026 (non-incumbent deadline)
- Voters' pamphlet statement deadline: August 25, 2026 ($300 fee)
- **Contribution limits**: $603 max per individual/political committee per election cycle
- **No expenditure limits** (struck down by Oregon Supreme Court)
- **No corporate/union/nonprofit direct contributions** — must form political committees
- **Small Donor Committees**: can give unlimited to candidates (cap $122/year per individual into the committee)
- Shannon Singleton won District 2 in 2024 with 54% (~18-20K votes); she's now running for County Chair, opening the seat
- 2024 general had ~30-35K total votes cast in District 2 commissioner race
- 2026 will likely have lower turnout (no presidential race) — estimate 20-25K votes, need ~10-13K to be competitive

## RCV Strategy

- Don't attack other candidates — be the acceptable second choice for everyone
- "I'd be fine with Nabil" is as valuable as "Nabil's my guy" under RCV
- Broad, non-polarizing positioning favors an experienced insider over an ideological candidate

## Design Tone

- **Confident, understated, editorial** — not campaign template, not grassroots movement
- Warm charcoal palette with warm ivory surfaces — no political party colors, no accent color
- Inter for all text; Libre Baskerville for endorsement quotes only
- Tight border radii (2-8px) — sharp, professional, not bubbly
- Minimal decoration — white space and typography carry the design
- No uppercase tracking-wide labels, no icons, no emoji
- Left-aligned throughout
- Monochrome buttons — the restraint IS the identity

## Content Principles

- Photos are the primary source of warmth and connection — the design stays neutral
- Community photos should reflect the district naturally — visibly diverse without being staged
- Use real North/Northeast Portland locations: Alberta, Mississippi, St. Johns
- His heritage is not a talking point through design — 30 years of experience is the lead
- Issues framing: not "what I believe" but "what I know" — insider knowledge, not promises
- No slogans, no "Forward Together" or "Vote for Change"
- Tone: direct, first-person where possible, sounds like a person not a campaign
- Issues should read like briefings, not platform documents

## Photography Direction

- **Hero**: b-roll video of District 2 — streets, bridges, neighborhoods, people. Not the candidate. The community is the subject.
- **About section**: personal headshot/portrait
- **Donate section**: full-width community photo (neighborhood, event, crowd) with dark overlay
- **Endorsements**: individual headshots of endorsers, square format with border

## Campaign Materials — Lean Approach

- **No mailers** — waste of money, goes straight in the trash
- **No door hangers** — same
- **No yard signs** (tentative) — marginal impact, sustainability angle
- **Recycled palm cards** — credit-card format, recycled PVC or eco-friendly material
  - Front: wordmark (Nabil for District 2), QR code to site
  - Back: "Pass this to a neighbor" or similar
  - QR code includes UTM parameters for tracking (utm_source=card&utm_medium=qr&utm_campaign=canvass)
  - Can vary utm_content by neighborhood for geographic tracking
  - Estimate: 3,000-5,000 cards
- **Digital ads** — targeted to District 2 zip codes during ballot window ($3-5K)
- **Voters' pamphlet statement** — $300 filing fee
- **Email list** — built via website signup, used sparingly (4-6 emails total across campaign)

## Website as Hub

- Every touchpoint loops back to the site: door knock → card → QR → site → email signup
- Issue pages are the conversion tool — real writing that demonstrates insider knowledge
- Blog/explainer posts serve dual purpose: voter education + SEO + social sharing
  - "Where your property taxes actually go"
  - "What a county commissioner actually does"
  - "How county services get funded and why some are failing"
- Email list built from site captures zip code for geographic targeting
- Vercel/Google Analytics tracks UTM sources — measures card scans, ad clicks, organic traffic

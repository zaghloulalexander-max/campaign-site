# Design & Campaign Notes

## Candidate

- **Nabil Zaghloul** — 30 years as a county executive, late 50s, looks relatively young
- Running for **Multnomah County Commissioner, District 2**
- Positioning: institutional knowledge + insider credibility to fix things from within
- Not a grassroots insurgent — a serious person with a record

## District 2 Demographics

- **North and Northeast Portland** — ~160,000 residents
- 65.4% White, 12.5% Hispanic, 8.4% Black, 7.4% Pacific Islander, 4.0% Asian
- Portland's most diverse district; historically the center of the Black community
- 61% Democrat, 29% non-affiliated, 5% Republican
- Median household income ~$94,000
- 14.4% of households speak a non-English language at home

## Design Tone

- **Confident, understated, editorial** — not campaign template, not grassroots movement
- Warm charcoal palette with warm ivory surfaces — no political party colors
- Inter for all text; Libre Baskerville for endorsement quotes only
- Tight border radii (2-8px) — sharp, professional, not bubbly
- Minimal decoration — white space and typography carry the design
- No uppercase tracking-wide labels, no icons, no emoji
- Left-aligned throughout

## Content Principles

- The candidate's name is foreign to most voters. The design should make it feel **normal and confident**, not exotic or decorative. No serif/display fonts on the name.
- Photos are the primary source of warmth and connection — the design stays neutral, photos provide the human element
- Community photos should reflect the district naturally — visibly diverse without being staged
- Use real North/Northeast Portland locations: Alberta, Mississippi, St. Johns
- His heritage is not a talking point through design — 30 years of experience is the lead
- Issues framing: not "what I believe" but "what I know" — insider knowledge, not promises

## Photography Direction

- **Hero**: environmental photo of Nabil — at an event, in a county building, talking to someone. Not a headshot.
- **About section**: personal headshot/portrait
- **Donate section**: full-width community photo (neighborhood, event, crowd) with dark overlay
- **Endorsements**: individual headshots of endorsers, square format with border
- **B-roll / video**: community footage — events, streets, local businesses. Not all clips need the candidate. The community is the subject.

## Copy Direction

- No slogans, no "Forward Together" or "Vote for Change"
- Tone: direct, first-person where possible, sounds like a person not a campaign
- Issues should read like briefings, not platform documents
- The word "support" is vague — avoid it as a section header
- "Donate" on buttons, not "Contribute" or "Give"
- "Volunteer" on buttons, not "Join" or "Get Involved"

## Future

- **Spanish language support** — route-based i18n via `[locale]` segment. Worth doing given 12.5% Hispanic population. Translate from finalized English content, not machine translation.
- **Events page** — add when the campaign is running regular public events
- **Detailed issues pages** — expand from one-liners to full positions when content is ready
- **Volunteer modal** — form triggered by button click, fields: first name, last name, email, phone (optional), zip, "how would you like to help?" checkboxes
- **Domain** — electnabil.com (short, memorable, action-oriented, not tied to specific district)
